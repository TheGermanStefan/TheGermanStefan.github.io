Definite & indefinite articles · Gender in German nouns

💡 Quick Tip: Every German noun has a gender: masculine (der), feminine (die), or neuter (das). Always learn the article together with the noun!
🔵 Maskulinum
der
ein / kein
der Mann, der Tisch
🔴 Femininum
die
eine / keine
die Frau, die Tür
🟢 Neutrum
das
ein / kein
das Kind, das Buch
📝 Plural: All genders → die (definite) / keine (indefinite negative). Example: die Männer, die Frauen, die Kinder

✅ Richtig oder Falsch?
Are the statements correct (R) or incorrect (F)?
#StatementR / F
1.In German, all plural nouns use „die" as the definite article.
2.„Eine" is the indefinite article for both feminine and neuter nouns.
3.„Das Mädchen" (the girl) is neuter in German, even though it refers to a female person.
4.The article changes to „einen" for masculine nouns in the accusative case (direct object).
5.German nouns can have grammatical gender that matches their natural gender (e.g. male/female).
