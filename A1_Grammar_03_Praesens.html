💡 Quick Tip: Regular German verbs follow a pattern: remove -en from the infinitive to get the stem, then add the correct ending. Most verbs follow this rule!

✅ Richtig oder Falsch?
Are the statements correct (R) or incorrect (F)?
#StatementR / F
1.The ending for „er/sie/es" in regular present tense is -t.
2.„Wir kaufen" and „sie kaufen" use the same form.
3.To conjugate a regular verb, you add the ending to the full infinitive.
4.„Du lernst" is the correct form for "you learn" (informal).
5.In German present tense, there is only one form for present actions (not a continuous form like in English).
