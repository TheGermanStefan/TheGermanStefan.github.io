✅ Richtig oder Falsch?
Are the statements correct (R) or incorrect (F)?
#StatementR / F
1.In German numbers 21–99, the ones digit always comes before the tens digit.
2.„Dreißig" (30) follows the regular -zehn pattern like dreizehn, vierzehn, etc.
3.The number 11 in German is „elf" — it does NOT follow the regular pattern.
4.„Sechzehn" means 16. The stem of „sechs" drops the -s before -zehn.
5.Telephone numbers in German are often read in pairs: 07 31 45 = null-sieben / einundthreißig / fünfundvierzig.
