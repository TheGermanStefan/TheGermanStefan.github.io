Die 4 Fälle (Kasus) The 4 cases — what they are and why they exist

💡 Was ist ein Kasus? — What is a case?

In German, nouns and articles change their form depending on their role in the sentence. Think of it as a label that tells you: "Is this word the actor? The thing being acted on? The recipient?"

Don't worry — at A0/A1 you only need to recognise them and use Nominativ + Akkusativ actively. Dativ comes at A2, Genitiv at B1.

Fragt: WER? WAS?

1. Nominativ

The subject — the person or thing doing the action.

Der Mann kauft Brot.
(The man buys bread.)

Fragt: WEN? WAS?

2. Akkusativ

The direct object — what or who receives the action directly.

Der Mann kauft den Apfel.
(The man buys the apple.)

Fragt: WEM?

3. Dativ

The indirect object — to whom or for whom something is done.

Ich gebe dem Kind ein Buch.
(I give the child a book.)

Fragt: WESSEN?

4. Genitiv

Shows possession — whose something is.

Das ist das Buch des Mannes.
(That is the man's book.)

🔍 Ein Satz — vier Rollen

See how the same sentence parts fit into the case system.

Nominativ (Wer?) → Verb → Akkusativ (Was?)
Der Hund frisst den Kuchen.
The dog (NOM) eats the cake (AKK).
Nominativ → Verb → Dativ (Wem?) → Akkusativ
Die Lehrerin gibt dem Kind ein Buch.
The teacher (NOM) gives the child (DAT) a book (AKK).

Artikel Definite (der/die/das) and indefinite (ein/eine) articles

Bestimmte Artikel (Definite: "the")

German has 3 genders: Maskulin (m) · Feminin (f) · Neutrum (n). The article tells you the gender.

Fall (Case)Maskulin (m)Feminin (f)Neutrum (n)Plural
Nominativ (subject)derdiedasdie
Akkusativ (direct obj.)den ⚠️diedasdie
Dativ (indirect obj.)demderdemden (+n)
Genitiv (possession)des (+s)derdes (+s)der

⚠️ Only maskulin accusativ changes: der → den. All others stay the same in NOM/AKK.

Unbestimmte Artikel (Indefinite: "a/an")

FallMaskulinFemininNeutrumPlural

Negativartikel: kein / keine

Used to negate a noun. Follows the same pattern as ein/eine.

Ich habe kein Auto.
I have no car. (Neutrum)
Das ist kein Problem.
That's not a problem.
Ich habe keine Zeit.
I have no time. (Feminin)
Er hat keinen Bruder.
He has no brother. (Mask. AKK)

Satzstellung Word order — the #1 rule in German

🔑 The Golden Rule: The verb is ALWAYS in position 2

No matter what comes first in the sentence, the verb stays in second place.

Normal order
Ichgeheheute nach Berlin.
Time word first (verb still 2nd!)
Heutegeheich nach Berlin.
Yes/No question → verb comes FIRST
Gehstdu nach Berlin?
W-Question → question word + verb + subject
Wohingehstdu?
